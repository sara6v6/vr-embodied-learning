# Research Design

## Motivation

This exploratory prototype examines how embodied interaction in a virtual learning environment may shape spatial presence, body ownership illusion, agency, cognitive engagement, and perceived learning. It connects educational technology with HCI, embodied cognition, immersive learning, and human augmentation.

The artifact makes a possible research design concrete. It is not a completed empirical study and does not claim validated or generalizable findings.

## Exploratory Research Questions

- **RQ1:** Does embodied avatar interaction increase exploratory behavior in a VR learning environment compared with a control condition without embodied affordances?
- **RQ2:** Do spatial presence and body ownership illusion correlate with self-reported cognitive engagement and perceived learning?
- **RQ3:** What behavioral interaction patterns emerge during embodied versus non-embodied VR exploration?

These questions are intended for small-scale pilot testing and design reflection, not statistically powered empirical claims.

## Between-Condition Comparison

The **embodied VR condition** includes first-person avatar hands attached to the camera, gaze-directed knowledge-card activation with subtle reach feedback, and brief hand feedback during teleportation. These affordances are designed to provide minimal browser-accessible body ownership and agency cues. They do not establish that a body ownership illusion occurs.

The **control condition** preserves the virtual classroom, learning topics, navigation quality, and card presentation while removing avatar hands, gaze activation, and teleportation. It remains a credible experience rather than a deliberately degraded comparison.

## Variables and Measures

| Category | Variables or measures |
| --- | --- |
| Condition | Embodied VR condition; control condition |
| Behavioral interaction log | Session duration, unique card count, card-open sequence, card-close events, teleportation count |
| Self-report instrument | VR familiarity, spatial presence, body ownership illusion, agency, cognitive engagement, emotional engagement, perceived learning, embodied interaction |

The instrument uses adapted exploratory items. It is not a complete validated psychometric scale.

## Intended Analysis

The current analysis is descriptive: counts, means, ranges, standard deviations when possible, and condition-level summaries. Any future inferential analysis would require an appropriate study protocol, adequate sample size, preregistered hypotheses where suitable, and careful instrument selection.

## Limitations

- Small sample size is expected during pilot testing.
- Browser-based WebVR may create lower presence than standalone head-mounted displays.
- Measures are self-report only; no physiological data is collected.
- Interaction occurs in a single session with no longitudinal follow-up.
- Demand characteristics and self-selection bias may influence responses.
- No formal ethics review has been conducted at this stage.
- localStorage can be cleared or unavailable.
- Survey items are adapted and not a complete validated psychometric instrument.
