# Ethics and Limitations

## Privacy and Ethics

- No personally identifiable information is requested or collected.
- All responses and behavioral interaction logs are stored locally in the browser.
- Nothing is transmitted to a server.
- The prototype has no login, tracking, analytics, or backend.
- Exported JSON should be anonymized and handled responsibly before analysis.
- This is not a formal human-subjects study and does not imply institutional approval.
- A future formal study would require appropriate consent, participant information, withdrawal procedures, data management, and institutional review where applicable.

The prototype is appropriate for design exploration and pilot testing. It is not appropriate for generalizable conclusions in its current form.

## Limitations

- Small sample size is expected.
- Browser-based WebVR may produce lower presence than standalone head-mounted displays.
- The self-report instrument uses adapted items and is not a complete validated psychometric instrument.
- No physiological or motion-capture measures are collected.
- Interaction is limited to a single session.
- No longitudinal data is available.
- Demand characteristics may influence self-report responses.
- Self-selection bias may affect any informal pilot sample.
- No formal ethics review has been conducted at this stage.
- localStorage content can be lost when browser data is cleared or storage is unavailable.
- Device, browser, display, and input differences may affect the between-condition comparison.

These limitations are part of transparent research positioning and identify concrete priorities for future work.
